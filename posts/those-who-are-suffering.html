#1

They look different from other people, both in how they present themselves to the outside and in their behaviour.

Those who are not only suffering, but who also are naturally inclined to handle themselves alone, are damned to stay in a constant state of alertness with respect to the external environment. Their endurance is what adds up the most to their pride and power. Having spent years isolated, they are able to stay religiously focused and unfazed in front of all incoming threats, to survive.

The reasons as to why they became who they are now are unclear and only speculative in nature. They could see life as it is, through the lens of brutal honesty and disillusion. These are things you do not choose to have but are born with or have nurtured within yourself unconsciously throughout the years.

Despite their questionable relationship with the outside world, they can somewhat maintain a balanced state of self being. Perhaps they have convinced themselves the future will be brighter, or maybe they are patiently waiting with hope for the future to be brighter. But what about the present?

The days go by, all dreams and aspirations slowly fade away. No reasonable amounts of commitment will help to fill the emptiness of the missing days. The state of reality does not freeze in time, it moves forward, there is no time left to take action.

#2

My eyes hurt from being exposed too much to artificial lights.

Tomorrow they will feel better, then worse again, and this same cycle will repeat. Tomorrow will be another day, and I will be writing again. It will be a day of deep reflection and spiritual cleanse from whatever is meaningless.

I wrote this to let you know the person that I am, or the person I want you to think of when you think about me. The human mind is strange in the sense of how it conceives things that are irrational and unrealistic to please our selfish desires. I believe the human ego is fragile because of how our mind pretends to trick itself to curb the emotional pain, to the point it needs to conceive things in a way that is pleasurable or to avoid suffering.

Maybe I'm just a bit of a neurotic, because sometimes I do think obsessively. Surprisingly, the sole act of thinking feels therapeutic in how it diverts my mind from dealing with the meaningless slush that life has become. I will keep thinking obsessively, but I will also try enjoying the seemingly-endless ride that is life. I will let free that flow of thoughts and not think too much about the rest.

#3

When they finally get past their sufferings, they will try to take over with sparkling energy. The same energy that moves all sorts of things in the physical world and lifts up human emotions, the engine of life.

But how could you blame them? The frustration and the negative thoughts boiled their blood for quite some time, and when all that mess releases itself from the body, it does so through art or revenge. It could manifest through art if their boredom has been the incubator for their frustration. Either way.. they have been obsessing too much!

No amounts of pain shall justify the actions of the perpetrator: to be morally corrupt is a conscious decision. Even though the individual is at his lowest, they must distinguish between the good and the evil. They ultimately knows which direction to take in life.

When those who are suffering finally break free of their living hell, they either become a bliss to this world or one of its nightmares, according to behavioural and cognitive science facts that I do not know anything about.

#4

If you are loved and everything's alright, why do all these negative thoughts come out at night? Where does the source of your distress come from?

Out of a place of confusion, the external environment feels strange. The streets you are familiar with may become unfamiliar, and the whole scenario dark, self-repeating, unstimulating. The entire environment comprised of places and people may even turn out to seem hostile. We will call "spiritual perdition" the human condition where a sense of estrangement and a combination of unsettling emotions cohexist together inside of the individual. Put on these terms, it is still unclear what this idea of perdition really is about.

A question that might pop up is if it relates to feelings of suffering within the individual. One observation is that the estrangement might trigger an urge for the search of answers and, lastly, the truth.

I'd like to believe the individual is self conscious the most when he is alone and by his thoughts. Or, as some would rightly point out, he may just turn crazy at some point, still according to behavioural and cognitive science facts I'm not aware of at the moment...

#5

Sometimes it's hard giving up your goals to maintain a moral balance. It seems reasonable to allow for a certain degree of separation from the ideal state of moral correctness.

If we imagine the possible states of moral behaviour to be part of a scale, we would put moral correctness on one side, and leave moral corruption on the other. We will then introduce a state that sits in the middle of the scale and call it "defiance", the state to which the absence of meaning in life has been a realization, and emotional numbness one of its consequences. It's neither a state to which being always "correct" is the standard, nor being "wrong" is not an option, but nevertheless, it marks a red line that should never be crossed for the purpose of achieving the end result.

Some people just get sick of keeping up with the bullshit. Their anger, suffering, and need for rebellion, become the raw material their moral system stems from. Their soul is not filled with hate, but they are rough enough to break away entirely from the foolishness infesting our society and detach themselves from conventional schemes and societal rules. Although their defiance doesn't save them from being perceived as an enemy, they ultimately are honest with their intentions.
